Payload Bone Acceleration

A collaborative project exploring the effects of high-frequency rocket launch vibrations on surgically repaired bones. Conducted with McGill’s Biology Department and engineering peers.

Team
McGill Rocket Team
Role
Hardware Engineer
Timeline
Ongoing – 2025
Tools
Altium, LTSpice, STM32, Soldering

Overview

This interdisciplinary research project investigates how surgically repaired bones — specifically those reinforced with screws — behave under rocket launch conditions. We aim to assess whether astronauts who have undergone bone surgery would experience structural issues in space missions.

Role & Contribution

I contributed to the hardware division by designing, assembling, and testing PCBs to collect vibration data using IEPE accelerometers. We processed and stored voltage data using STM32 microcontrollers with SD card logging.

Thermal Integration

A parallel aspect of the project involved regulating bone temperature using Peltier Coolers. I helped analyze cooling effectiveness and contributed to the circuit design required to integrate thermal regulation into our existing PCB system.

Technical Challenges

PCB trace routing was demanding due to space constraints and signal path integrity. I assisted in repositioning components, layering polygon pours, and troubleshooting via connections to achieve a reliable layout.

Reflection

This project taught me the full electrical hardware workflow — from schematic capture to soldering and testing — and reinforced the importance of collaboration across disciplines in solving biomedical and aerospace problems.
