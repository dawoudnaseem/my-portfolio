Project Case Study

Payload Bone Acceleration

Overview: This interdisciplinary research project was developed in collaboration with McGill’s Biology Department and fellow engineering students to investigate how surgically repaired bones — specifically those connected with screws — respond to high-frequency vibrations during rocket launches.

Role & Responsibilities: As part of the hardware division, I worked on PCB design, soldering, and testing. Our system used an IEPE accelerometer and STM32 microcontroller to gather and store acceleration data in an SD card. I also assisted with integrating a Peltier Cooler system to regulate temperature for testing bone stability.

Technical Tools: Altium Designer, LTSpice, STM32, IEPE Accelerometer, SD card logging, soldering techniques, and component sourcing from DigiKey.

Design Process: Focused on multi-layer PCB layout and trace routing, resolving component placement issues and managing signal paths using vias and polygon pours for electrical integrity.

Status: The first PCB iteration is printed and currently undergoing testing. If successful, final assembly and system integration will follow.

Reflection: This project helped me experience the full design cycle — from schematics to soldering to debugging — and strengthened my ability to collaborate across disciplines, especially with biology and mechanical engineering teammates.
